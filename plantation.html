The National Service Scheme (NSS), launched in 1969, embodies the motto "Not Me, But You," fostering youth engagement in community service and social welfare. At HBTU, NSS promotes leadership, civic responsibility, and holistic development. Rooting for a Greener Tomorrow: College Plantation Drive in Action

September 22, 2024 | 10:00 AM
Authored by: Samriddhi jain, BBA

“A nation that destroys its soil destroys itself . Forests are lungs of our land, purifying the air and giving fresh strength to our people"- by Franklin D. Roosevelt emphasises on the vital role of nature, particularly forests and healthy soils, sustaining life and well-being of a nation. The message is clear : protecting and nurturing nature is essential for our health, strength and future. Recognising this, the national service scheme under the leadership of Student Secretary, Aryan Tiwari actively organizes plantation drives to protect and rejuvenate the environment. These initiatives embody the essence of the quote by promoting afforestation, improving air quality and raising awareness about the importance of trees in sustaining life.

On 13 August 2024, the HBTU community came together under the banner of Azadi ka Amrit mahotsav to contribute to a sustainable future by organising a successful plantation drive at West campus . In the commemoration of 75 years of independence, students, volunteers and faculty members, collectively contributed to environmental conservation by planting saplings as a gesture of giving back to nature.

NSS organizes plantation drives to promote environmental sustainability and raise awareness about climate change. These initiatives help combat air pollution, support biodiversity, prevent soil erosion, and mitigate climate change by increasing green cover. In addition to environmental benefits, the drives foster community involvement and environmental responsibility among youth, contributing to long-term ecological and social well-being.

The NSS motto, "Not Me, But You," reflects the spirit of plantation drives, where the focus is on collective well-being rather than individual gain. These drives promote environmental sustainability and community responsibility, with volunteers selflessly working for the greater good—protecting the planet for future generations. A special mention for Aryan Tiwari and team for hosting these drives and making it a massive success.

Project Highlights

  • Planted trees to combat climate change, enhance air quality, and preserve biodiversity.
  • Fostered ecological awareness and responsibility
  • Over 80 Saplings planted by students and faculty.

"Seeing so many hands come together to plant trees fills me with hope and pride. Let’s continue this mission to create a healthier environment for future generations!"
